    • About Me

    Welcome! My name is Shirui, or Ryan, and this is my personal website. I started this website in the summer of 2015 as a project for me to learn the basics of web development (HTML, CSS, jQuery, Javascript) and User Experience Design. I am currently a student enrolled in the Software Engineering program at the University of Waterloo in Waterloo, Canada. I love technology, and I want to be able to develop software that works together with hardware to enhance and enrich peoples' daily lives.

    I've been fascinated by computers ever since I was a child. I would read magazines about both computer hardware and software, and then experiment with various how-to guides such as system optimizaton or overclocking. I built my first PC when I was 10, after selecting and ordering parts online. I first started to dabble in programming when I was in Grade 9 for one of my school courses, and I realized I really enjoyed it. Since then, I've become experienced at both Java and C through personal projects and school courses. One of my current goals is to gain more experience with mobile app and web development.

    While technology fascinates me and is the career I want to pursue, one of my longtime hobbies has been music, specficially the piano. I've studied the piano for over 12 years, and I still continue to play currently. It is something I enjoy immensely and am passionate about. I completed all the theory and practical requirements for the RCM ARCT's Diploma in Piano Performance in 2014. I've performed at numerous concerts and also participated in competitions as a solo pianist.

    In addition to the piano, I picked up the Clarinet while in elementary school and continued to play it all the way through high school. I've performed on clarinet too, whether it be in the school wind ensemble or as a part of a chamber ensemble. In high school, I also played piano in the jazz band as well as accompanied the school choir. I was also on my school Music Council for three years, serving as a Vice-President in my last year.

    I'm a lover of Classical, Jazz, Broadway, and Film music. In particular, I love music of the Romantic Era, such as Chopin, Beethoven, and Liszt. I also love reading books, especially thrillers and classics. Some personal favourites are The Count of Monte Cristo, Gone Girl, and The Godfather. Some of my other interests include modern physics such as relativity, quantum mechanics, and string theory.

    This award is given to the student of the graduating class who obtained the highest average throughout. I received this award in June of 2015 during my high school graduation.

    YRDSB Student Trustee Award for Character

    This award is given to a student of the graduating class who has exhibited leadership and positive character attributes in school, acting as a role model for others. I received this award in June of 2015 during graduation.

    From grades 10 - 12, I received the Computer Science award as the student in the class with the highest mark in the course.

    RCM ARCT Diploma in Piano Performance

    I received this diploma after completing all of the practical and theory requirements in the Royal Conservatory of Music's syballus in 2014.

    York Region Celebration of the Arts Bursary Recipient

    I was selected as a Bursary Recipient in the Celebration of the Arts contest in 2013. This was a contest held in York Region, where a video entry was submitted. Amongst these entries, winners were selected by a panel of judges. The contest culminated in a concert for all the bursary recipients in October of 2013.

    Volunteer Experience

    Teacher Assistant

    I assisted the teacher in the International Languages program at my school which was run weekly. I was placed in a class with students learning Mandarin, since I myself am fluent in Mandarin, and I would assist the teacher with various tasks such as organization of handouts, assisting students with their work, as well as general cleanup and supervision.

    Musician for School Musical Production

    When I was in Grade 11, I was involved with my school's music and drama department in the production of the musical Olivier. I decided to volunteer as a member of the Pit Orchestra as a pianist. From October to April, we had weekly 2 hour rehearsals in preparation for the week's worth of performances in late April. It was a wonderful, rich, and challenging time that I enjoyed greatly.
